INTRODUCTION

The analysis reveals significant trends in urban land dynamics, highlighting the importance of geometric assessments in urban planning. Kolkata's urban expansion shows dominant northward growth (peaking at 100 units), with moderate eastern and western development, while southern sectors (south/southwest/southeast) remain largely stagnant. This north-centric pattern likely reflects infrastructure development along the Eastern Metropolitan Bypass and new town projects, with constrained southern growth due to the Hooghly River and existing urban density. The trend suggests focused northern expansion requiring balanced regional planning. (Units likely represent km² or % change.)
